Payroll Method Selection

ASK Staffing offers two options for receiving your paycheck. Please choose from one of the options below and sign and date.

Direct Deposit — Paychecks will be automatically deposited into the employee's bank account when a voided check is provided by the employee. Employees will be able to view and/or print their paystubs from ADP Online Payroll site at https://workforcenow.adp.com/public/index.htm. Before your first paycheck date you will receive a welcome letter via email from ASK Staffing with a temporary login and password. You will be given the opportunity to change your password after your first login.
The voided check or direct deposit form from the employee's banking institution is mandatory for participation in direct deposit. Please note, by not providing a voided check or direct deposit form from your banking institution you are fully responsible for the $25.00 FEE charged by the payroll company for every direct deposit returned due to closed accounts and incorrect routing or account numbers supplied by the employee.
Payroll Card — Employees wishing to take advantage of direct deposit without the use of a bank account can elect to receive a Skylight Financial payroll card. The temporary payroll card will be enrolled and sent by priority mail with the first paper paycheck. There is a one-time $10.00 fee for a payroll card and mailing, which will be deducted from the first paycheck when the card is mailed out. Once the employee receives the card in the mail, he/she must activate the card by calling the activation number on the card. All future paychecks will be deposited to the payroll card beginning with your second paycheck.
***When assignment ends for any reason (i.e. termination, resignation or assignment complete) Final paycheck will/may be issued as paper check NOT direct deposit. *** Please note: If a paper check lost in the mail or not received due to an incorrect address provided by you, there will be a $25.00 stop payment fee to reissue your check. ASK Staffing is not responsible for any delays by the US Postal service, or any delivery issues at your location.
